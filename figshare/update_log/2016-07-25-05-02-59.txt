[u'Demographic and Clinical Factors Associated With Verbal Memory Performance in Patients with Schizophrenia in Hospital Universiti Sains Malaysia (HUSM), Malaysia', ['Zahiruddin Othman', 'Ruzita Jamaluddin', 'Muhammad Najib MA', 'Hasanah CI'], u'2016-07-25T02:43:23Z', u'Objective: The present study aims to assess verbal\nmemory performance in patients with schizophrenia attending HUSM and determine\nthe relationship between the patients\u2019 verbal memory performance and their\ndemographic/clinical factors. Methods: A cross sectional study of 114\npatients with schizophrenia attending HUSM psychiatric services from December\n2007 to May 2008 was conducted. The schizophrenia symptoms as well as verbal\nmemory performance were assessed using the Brief Psychiatric Rating Scale, the\nMalay version of the Calgary Depression Scale (MVCDS), and the Malay version of\nthe Auditory Verbal Learning Test (MVAVLT). The relationship between verbal\nmemory performance and demographic/clinical symptoms was evaluated using\nPearson Correlation. Results: Overall MVAVLT scores in all the trials\nwere lowered in patients with schizophrenia compared to average healthy\ncontrols. There were significant relationships between occupational status and\nMVAVLT performance in Trial A1-A5 Total; between educational level and MVAVLT\nperformance in Trial A1 and Trial A1-A5 Total and between severities of\nillness; and MVAVLT performance in all indexes except Trial A1 after controlled\nfor occupation and educational level. Conclusions: Patient with\nschizophrenia in HUSM performed significantly worse than healthy controls in\nverbal memory with or without interference. There were significant\nrelationships between MVAVLT performance and patient\u2019s occupational status,\neducational level and severity of the illness but not depressive symptoms.', u'https://figshare.com/articles/Demographic_and_Clinical_Factors_Associated_With_Verbal_Memory_Performance_in_Patients_with_Schizophrenia_in_Hospital_Universiti_Sains_Malaysia_HUSM_Malaysia/3497411', [u'Psychiatry (incl.
